Final Project - Spotlight on America: Identifying Optimal and Challenging Locations

This dashboard presents visualizations that provide insights into the overall scores across various states in the United States. The overall score is calculated based on several key factors, including:

  • Population Data on Cities:
    • Economic Opportunities: Higher populations often correlate with more job opportunities and economic activities.
    • Infrastructure and Services: Larger cities typically have better infrastructure and access to services like healthcare, education, and public transportation.
    • Social Dynamics: Understanding the population size helps in assessing community diversity and social opportunities.
  • Average Temp per State:
    • Regional Climate Variations: The map highlights the differences in average temperatures across states, which can influence regional climate characteristics and seasonal weather patterns.
    • Temperature Trends Over Time: The line graph shows historical temperature trends, providing insights into how average temperatures have changed over the decades and indicating potential climate change effects.
    • Impact on Local Environments: Understanding these temperature trends is crucial for anticipating impacts on local ecosystems, wildlife, and vegetation, helping to inform conservation efforts and environmental planning.
  • Crime Rate per Cities:
    • Safety and Security: Crime rates directly impact the safety and security of residents, influencing where people choose to live.
    • Property Values: High crime rates can decrease property values, while lower crime rates can make areas more attractive for investment.
    • Community Well-being: Low crime rates contribute to a sense of community well-being and quality of life.
  • Descending and Ascending Population Growth:
    • Economic Trends: Population growth trends indicate economic vitality or decline, helping to identify emerging or struggling regions.
    • Urban Planning: Insights into population changes aid in urban planning and the allocation of resources for sustainable development.
    • Real Estate Market: Population growth influences housing demand and real estate market trends, guiding investment decisions.
  • Cost of Living:
    • Affordability: Understanding the cost of living is crucial for assessing the affordability of a location, impacting financial planning for individuals and families.
    • Comparison of Regions: Cost of living data allows for comparisons between different regions, helping people make informed decisions about relocation.
    • Economic Viability: It provides insights into the economic viability of an area, influencing business investments and personal financial stability.
